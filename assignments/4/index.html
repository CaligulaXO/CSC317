About Me

Hello, My name is Ethan M. Garcia. I am a student at San Francisco State University. I am 22 years old and I am very passionate about learning new things. When I was a kid, I would watch Animal Planet with my dad which got me into learning about reptiles and insects. My favorites would change like the seasons, my first favorite lizard was the Gila Monster, then it switched to the Green Pit Viper, and if I were to list the rest, this webpage would look like an Obelisk full of venomous snakes and spiders. As I grew older, I realized that I worked well with computers. With the help of my mother, I developed my understanding of computers during middle school. It began with us fixing an old laptop that my uncle gave us. We were able to reinstall the Windows OS and reconfigure the default language (Italian) into English. From there, we took math courses seriously. My mother and I would watch Algebra II and Pre-Calc videos on youtube. I felt like I would be a great computer engineer.

After entering college, I found it a bit difficult to stay motivated to continue my major. There were a few professors that really made me want to quit this major however, Professors like Yash Gandhi, James Wong, Jeonghee Kim, and Duc Ta inspired me to continue on the path that I am on today. Continuing on, another Professor that inspired me to expand my career horizons was professor Amedeo Simoncini. The class he taught was ENGR 610, "Engineering Cost Analysis", that class combinded the business aspect of engineering. I really enjoyed being in the class, which may be surprising since the entire grade was based on exams (3 total with the weight of each being 33%). It was MS Excel focused, which gave me a feeling of familiarity since my mother had a "Business-focused" career. It was a class that gave me some sort of purpose with major.
